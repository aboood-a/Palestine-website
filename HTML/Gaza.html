The Occupation with Gaza

Gaza has been surrounded and harassed by the occupation for decades, with severe restrictions on movement, trade, and access to basic necessities. The blockade imposed by the occupation controls Gaza‚Äôs borders, airspace, and maritime access, limiting imports of food, fuel, medical supplies, and construction materials. The people of Gaza live under constant threat of airstrikes, ground incursions, and surveillance, creating fear and instability in everyday life. The occupation has led to significant economic challenges, with high unemployment rates, limited job opportunities, and widespread poverty. Many families struggle to meet their basic needs.

Current Crisis: Bombings and Humanitarian Emergency

Gaza is being bombed relentlessly at this very moment, with airstrikes targeting homes, hospitals, schools, and markets. Thousands of civilians, including children, women, and the elderly, have been killed or injured. The destruction of infrastructure has left most of Gaza without access to electricity, clean water, or functioning hospitals. Food scarcity is critical; families are struggling to find enough to eat, and humanitarian aid is insufficient to meet the urgent needs. Despite the devastation, Gazans continue to survive, resist, and support one another, showing courage under unimaginable circumstances.
